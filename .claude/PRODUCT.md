# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young adults and adults (18–35, skews male) in Argentina interested in starting or deepening calisthenics and street workout training. They range from absolute beginners to advanced athletes. Context: browsing on mobile or desktop, deciding whether to commit to a first class. They want proof that this is a real, serious community — not a corporate chain and not a soft wellness studio.

## Product Purpose

Landing page for TS Calisthenics Academy. Converts visitors into first-class reservations. Secondary goals: build trust through coaches, real photos, and community challenge content; drive app downloads and WhatsApp contact. Success = someone tapping "Reservar primera clase" or messaging via WhatsApp.

## Brand Personality

Cruda, intensa, comunidad — Raw, intense, community. The brand voice is direct and uncompromising. No corporate softness, no motivational poster aesthetics. The tone says: your body is the machine, the gym is the street, and the tribe holds you to account.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate gym chains** (Megatlón, SmartFit, Gold's Gym): generic blue/red palettes, stock photos of equipment, franchise vibe. TS is the opposite.
- **Soft fitness apps** (Nike Training Club, Freeletics consumer): pastel colors, cursive motivational quotes, wellness/yoga softness, rounded bright UI. None of that.

## Design Principles

1. **Rawness is the brand** — Rough edges, heavy typography, and real unretouched photos over polished perfection. If something looks too corporate, strip it back.
2. **The body is the machine** — No equipment photography. Humans, movement, and texture. Copy reinforces what the body can do without machines.
3. **Community over service** — This is a tribe, not a subscription. Language and visuals show people training together, coaches who are also athletes.
4. **Dark intensity as baseline** — The near-black surface is load-bearing atmosphere. The orange accent earns its place by being used sparingly; diluting it with gradients or decorative overuse kills the contrast.
5. **Convert through conviction** — Every section moves the visitor toward one decision: show up for the first class. No feature tours, no comparison tables.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum. Body text must be ≥4.5:1 contrast against background.
- All muted gray (#888888) text against #0a0a0a (~5.9:1) passes AA. Verify before adding lighter grays.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all CSS animations (marquee, flip cards).
- No accessibility requirements stated beyond standard web — no known user-specific needs.
